## Voice and persona

- **The practitioner-teacher who has earned the right to talk.** The author consistently writes from direct experience, framing hard-won knowledge as a gift to the reader: "evite alguns dos gargalos mais comuns no desenvolvimento de libs, poupando um bom tempo de sofrimento que eu mesmo tive." The tone is collegial, not authoritative — the author presents themselves as someone who also got burned, not someone who always knew better.

- **Self-aware geekiness worn as a badge.** The author explicitly labels their own identity: "a frustração de um adulto geek de meia-idade que não consegue completar o álbum." This self-deprecation is not false modesty — it anchors the reader in a shared subculture (programming, games, pop culture references) and makes technical content feel approachable.

- **Direct address to a named reader.** The author routinely invokes a specific imagined reader — "Saudações, Pythonista!", "meu caro amigo Pythonista", "E aí, bora começar?" — and uses the second person as a structuring device, not just a courtesy. This creates a one-on-one tutorial register even in long-form technical articles.

- **Comic self-disclosure as a rhetorical move.** The author volunteers their own mistakes and frustrations — "não repetir as minhas cag*$@s", "Eu esperando os testes de integração rodarem" — as teaching moments. This is a deliberate pattern, not accidental candor.

- **Enthusiasm for interdisciplinary bridging.** The author takes evident pleasure in connecting disparate domains: probability theory and Harry Potter, MLOps and canary coal miners, signal processing and CNNs, Bruno Latour's Actor-Network Theory and technical onboarding. This cross-domain enthusiasm is a signature intellectual move.

- **Academic background that surfaces in informal writing.** The doctoral thesis on social theory (Actor-Network Theory, Fairclough's Critical Discourse Analysis) trained habits that persist in blog writing: careful definition of terms before using them, attention to how language frames problems, and a tendency to zoom out to epistemological stakes before zooming back in.

## Sentence and paragraph structure

- **Medium-to-long paragraphs with a clear internal logic: setup, elaboration, punchline or pivot.** Paragraphs rarely have fewer than three sentences and often end with either a humorous aside or a bridge sentence that propels forward. The final sentence often carries the argumentative payload.

- **Frequent use of rhetorical questions as section openers or transitions.** "Mas será que esta é a melhor maneira de se fazer isso?", "Qual arquivo deve ser executado primeiro?", "E aí, bora começar?" These are not decorative — they signal a topic shift and invite the reader to track the author's reasoning.

- **Lists used architecturally, not decoratively.** Bullet and numbered lists appear when the content is genuinely enumerable (prerequisites, steps, command flags), but the surrounding prose always contextualizes the list before and after. Lists are never used to replace argument.

- **Parenthetical asides embedded in sentences, often carrying the humor or the caveat.** Examples: "(isso também é conhecido por soma harmônica)", "(a galera de produto deve estar pirando agora)", "(por increça que parível)". These parentheticals are a structural tic — the author maintains forward momentum in the main clause while inserting color or qualification in the aside.

- **Connective tissue that bridges sections explicitly.** The author consistently uses bridge sentences — "Feita a piada de tiozão. Bora lá?", "Com isso, fechamos o ciclo" — that mark the end of one movement and the beginning of the next. Section transitions are never abrupt.

- **In technical posts, code blocks and prose alternate in a call-and-response pattern.** Code is introduced, shown, and then explained line-by-line in prose. The author never drops code without immediately walking through what it does.

## Openings and closings

- **Openings begin with a scene, a personal anecdote, or a provocative rhetorical question — not a definition.** The Harry Potter post opens mid-scene: "Quando comecei a colecionar as figurinhas do álbum do Harry Potter lançado pela rede Savegnago de supermercados, logo percebi que minha jornada mágica escondia um lado sombrio." The randomness post opens with "Imagine if much of the patterns we perceive...".

- **Opening paragraphs set a contract with the reader.** By the end of the first three paragraphs, the author always states what the article will and will not do: "Ao final desta leitura, espero que você: (1)...(2)...(3)...". This is a deliberate structuring habit.

- **Closings are warm, brief, and sometimes playfully anti-climactic.** "Brincadeiras à parte, espero que o artigo tenha sido valioso e informativo para você. Até a próxima!", "É isso, meu caro amigo Pythonista! Obrigado pela atenção e nos vemos na próxima ;)", "Por hoje é só, meus amigos." The author does not attempt grand conclusions — they wrap up, thank the reader, and leave.

- **Technical posts close with a forward pointer to a sequel or related topic.** "No próximo post, quero te mostrar como usar seus conhecimentos de Shell para baixar dados e interagir com APIs diretamente da linha de comando." This serialization instinct shapes even standalone articles into episodes of an ongoing conversation.

- **The closing often echoes or resolves the opening metaphor.** The Harry Potter post opens and closes with the Gringotts gold metaphor. The randomness post opens and closes with humility about pattern-perception. This circularity is a recurring structural choice.

- **Pop-culture comparisons deployed to reduce intimidation around technical concepts.** "Se você pensou na CNN do logo acima [the news network], desculpe desapontá-lo" (on Convolutional Neural Networks). The author uses familiar reference points as on-ramps to unfamiliar terrain.

- **Parenthetical jokes that break the fourth wall.** "(ba-dum-tss)" after a pun about CNNs not transmitting news. "(maybe an all-you-can-eat celestial buffet)" in the Pascal's Wager section. These asides acknowledge the reader is aware this is a performance, which creates intimacy.

- **The author names their own Portuguese-language colloquialisms as a register marker.** "artigo bem 'nerdola'", "'colinha oficial'", "mequetréfi", "cookiecutter Tabajara". The word "Tabajara" (Brazilian slang for improvised/makeshift) appears across multiple posts as a running self-deprecating label for the author's own tools.

- **Ironic understatement applied to genuinely hard problems.** "Isso evitaria uma boa dose de dor de cabeça." "Complexo? Um pouco." The deflation of difficulty signals confidence, not dismissiveness.

- **Meme images and captions function as a punchline delivery system.** Captions like "Harry assustado com o valor para completar o álbum", "O projeto de .sh mais lindo da internet" show that the author treats captions as comedic beats, not descriptions.

## Lexical fingerprint

- **Brazilian tech jargon used unselfconsciously, without scare quotes.** Terms like "textão", "bora", "lib", "steira de CI/CD", "cookiecutter", "datamesh" appear in plain text. The author assumes a reader who lives in this world.

- **Repeated use of specific intensifiers:** "bem" (often warm-redundant: "bem conhecida", "bem simples"), "bastante" (preferred over "muito" in technical contexts), "de fato" (to emphasize genuine claims: "aprender alguma coisa, de fato"). These are habitual, not deliberate variation.

- **"Pragmático/pragmática" as a positive value marker.** "a escolha mais pragmática", "Pascal was a pragmatic thinker." Pragmatism is the author's implicit ethical standard for technical decisions.

- **"Contudo, entretanto, todavia" appear in clusters as a stylistic tic.** In the AWS post: "Contudo, entretanto, todavia, esse tipo de import declarativo só funciona bem quando..." This triple conjunction is a deliberate ironic flourish.

- **The closing greeting formula is stable:** "Saudações, pessoal! Tudo tranquilo?" / "Saudações, Pythonista!" at openings, and "Até a próxima!" / "Até lá!" at sign-offs.

- **Technical terms inside prose are always backtick-formatted and explained in context.** The author never drops `os.getenv()` without telling you what it does. This discipline carries over into prose: technical terms are contextualized on first use.

## Argumentation style

- **Problem-first, solution-second, with the problem stated as a felt experience.** Before explaining the Coupon Collector's Problem, the author describes the frustration of spending too much on sticker packs. Before explaining Makefiles, the author describes returning to old code and finding it incomprehensible. The felt problem legitimizes the technical solution.

- **Arguments are built by progressive disclosure, not by front-loading conclusions.** The CNN post walks through continuous convolution, then discrete convolution, then cross-correlation, before revealing that CNNs don't implement true convolution. The reader arrives at the surprising conclusion through a guided journey.

- **Counterintuitive claims are marked, set up, and then resolved.** "Mas não se desespere! O que você viu até agora não será perdido." "Wrong. The roulette wheel has no memory." These reversals are clearly signposted.

- **Explicit statement of what the text will NOT cover.** "não vou dar tanta ênfase aqui", "é importante mencionar que este artigo não substitui a leitura completa". The author sets scope boundaries clearly — an academic writing habit that persists in blog format.

- **Conclusions are framed as management of uncertainty, not resolution of it.** "Deploying models is, above all, an exercise in risk management." "The solution? Develop a more skeptical outlook." The author rarely claims to have solved problems definitively.

## Context-specific shifts

In English-language posts, humor is more restrained but structural habits (rhetorical questions, parenthetical asides, image captions as punchlines) persist.

### Academic / formal

The thesis introduction (pages 16–20) reveals the academic register in full. Key features:

- **First-person plural as epistemic humility.** The author writes "Nossa primeira hipótese postula", "acreditamos que", "nossa tarefa remanescente" — the collective "we" marks the research as a shared scholarly enterprise, not a personal claim. This contrasts sharply with the blog's singular "eu".

- **Citation density is structural, not ornamental.** Nearly every claim has a parenthetical citation. This creates a rhythm of assertion-then-anchor: "a compreensão de como as estruturas de poder são construídas, contestadas e reproduzidas reside precisamente na interação entre a semiótica das palavras e a materialidade (Foucault, 1996)." The author uses citations as load-bearing brackets, not decorative footnotes.

- **Footnotes carry the explanatory burden for theoretical asides.** Rather than interrupting the main argument, the author moves all definitional elaboration (e.g., full explanations of what Bourdieu means by "campo do poder") to numbered footnotes. This keeps the body text argumentatively lean while providing depth for readers who need it.

- **Explicit gap-mapping before stating objectives.** The introduction systematically identifies three lacunas in the literature before stating the research objective — this is a deliberate rhetorical structure, not just convention. The author names the gap, names its consequence, then positions the work as the response.

- **Colloquial connectors bleed into formal prose.** "Pois bem, após examinar brevemente o lócus teórico e empírico desta tese, estamos agora posicionados para definir..." — "Pois bem" is a conversational transition that belongs in speech or blog writing. The author permits this register leak at key argumentative pivots, suggesting it is an intentional softening of the academic voice.

- **Numbered inline enumeration for multi-part claims.** "os 'porta-vozes' (Latour, 2005) de um oligopólio tecnológico com capacidade para: (i) mudar toda a comunicação digital; (ii) extinguir e criar milhões de empregos; (iii) erradicar doenças por meio da filantropia..." — this roman-numeral inline list is the academic equivalent of the blog's bullet points. The structural instinct is identical across registers.

- **Self-disclosure of learning goals even in academic context.** "O primeiro artigo foi um exercício metodológico com o objetivo de dominar algumas ferramentas de PLN e integrá-las..." — the author admits personal skill-building as a legitimate research motivation. This transparency about intellectual formation is unusual in academic writing and mirrors the blog's self-disclosure of learning-by-doing.

- **Epigraph choice signals intellectual persona.** The thesis opens with a Frank Herbert (*Dune*) quote: *"Nas profundezas do inconsciente humano reside uma necessidade vaga por um universo lógico que faça sentido. Mas o universo real está sempre um passo à frente da lógica."* Selecting speculative fiction for a social science thesis is a deliberate statement of interdisciplinary identity — the same instinct that drives blog posts to open with Harry Potter or Magic: The Gathering.

### Data analysis / critical opinion (new register — wip/new_article.md)

The State of Data article reveals a third register not present in tutorial posts or the thesis: **data-driven critical analysis with an explicit personal stake**. Key features that differentiate this mode:

- **The author shifts from "practitioner-teacher" to "informed insider with an opinion."** Phrases like "E aí, bora lá?" and "eu mesmo fui um caso de absorção por este mercado" position the author as someone who is both analyzing and being analyzed. The article is not tutorial (here's how to do X) nor academic (here's the literature on X) — it's a argued reading of evidence by someone living the phenomenon.

- **Data is never cited without being interpreted.** Every statistic is immediately followed by a "o que isso significa" move: "Esse número, embora alto, é o menor desde 2021 — o mercado parece estar se estabilizando." The author refuses to be a passive reporter; every number gets a verdict.

- **Sections open with a named tension, not just a topic.** Instead of "Remuneração" as a section header, the article uses moves like "O debate do trabalho remoto: uma tensão que não vai embora." The section title announces the argumentative angle, not just the subject matter.

- **Personal anecdote is used to validate external data, not to replace it.** "Trazendo para a minha experiência pessoal (afinal, também fui respondente), eu mesmo fui um caso de absorção por este mercado..." The author deploys first-person testimony as a data point alongside institutional data — IBGE, Kaggle, Stack Overflow — creating a dual-track argument (macro evidence + micro witness).

- **Comparative framing with global benchmarks is a deliberate structural choice.** The author consistently answers an implicit "mas o Brasil é fora da curva?" by comparing with equivalent international surveys. This move preempts the reader's counterargument and positions the author as genuinely informed.

- **"O dado mais revelador" and similar superlative frames are a rhetorical signature in this mode.** "O dado mais revelador, porém, não é quem entrevistou — é quem está aberto a propostas sem nem estar procurando ativamente." These superlative setups signal that the author has ranked the evidence by importance, not just summarized it.

- **The author names the systemic implication before the reader can dismiss the data as anomaly.** "Para as empresas, isso significa que a retenção não é um problema só de RH — é uma questão estratégica permanente." This preemptive generalization is characteristic of the author's critical analysis mode.

- **"O que o relatório diz nas entrelinhas" is a recurring deep-reading move.** The author goes beyond the explicit findings to interpret the structural patterns behind the data — a habit rooted in critical discourse analysis training.

## Review guidance

When `--preserve-voice` is active, apply these rules:

**Flag as voice erasure (do not change):**
- Removal of direct address forms: "bora!", "fechou?", "Vamos lá?", "Saudações, Pythonista!"
- Removal of parenthetical jokes or fourth-wall breaks embedded in technical sentences
- Replacing colloquial Brazilian terms ("textão", "nerdola", "Tabajara", "bora", "rolou") with formal equivalents
- Flattening the triple-adversative construction ("Contudo, entretanto, todavia") — it is intentional irony
- Removing first-person confessions of difficulty or failure — these are structural, not accidental
- Cutting forward-pointer closing sentences that tease the next episode
- Changing "bora" to "vamos" or similar — the author consistently uses the contracted register
- Removing image captions that deliver humor — these are part of the content architecture
- Stripping institutional source characterization from opening paragraphs to "tighten the hook" — the author wants the source named and qualified even in the opening; credibility anchors are not decoration
- Removing the author's casual continuity connectors ("Não muito diferente do que rolou nos últimos anos") — these set a deliberate "same story, different year" register
- Replacing colloquial list-closers like "continuou na mesma" with formal summaries — the deflation is intentional
- Removing embedded anglicisms used without scare quotes ("remote-first", "trends") — the author uses English terms when the Portuguese equivalent would sound more formal or imprecise
- Simplifying "bora lá mergulhar nesses números?" to "bora lá?" — the extended formula with action verb is a richer, deliberate version

**Flag as voice inconsistency (worth surfacing to author):**
- Use of formal register markers ("ademais", "outrossim", "conforme supracitado") that never appear in the corpus
- Passive constructions that erase the author as actor — even in the thesis, the author uses active "nós" rather than passivized constructions
- Overly long paragraphs without a clear pivot or forward momentum at the end
- Sections that skip gap-stating or felt-problem setup and go straight to the solution
- Missing bridge sentences at section transitions
- English text significantly more formal than the Drunkard's Walk piece (the benchmark for the author's English register)
- Academic text that omits self-disclosure of the author's own stake or learning journey — this is part of the author's academic voice, not unprofessional candor
- Removal of colloquial connectors ("Pois bem", "bora lá") at argumentative pivots — these are intentional register softeners, not editing oversights

**Always acceptable to revise:**
- Spelling and typographical errors
- Factual inaccuracies in technical claims (command flags, AWS service names, mathematical formulas)
- Broken internal links or references to non-existent sections
- Code blocks with syntax errors or non-functional examples
- Structural redundancy where the same point is made in consecutive paragraphs without addition
- Captions that are factually wrong about what an image shows (replacing a descriptive caption with a comedic one would be voice erasure, but fixing a factually wrong caption is always acceptable)
